Background Information

Education & Reference

Tracheostomy Plug

The obstruction of the tracheostomy tube as a result of a mucus plug, secretions, kinked tube or even a small object.

Contributing Factors

  • Dehydration
  • Inadequate humidification of the airway
  • Inadequate or infrequent pulmonary toilet
  • Intervals between routine trach tube changes too long

Symptoms

  • Increased WOB
  • Audible congestion
  • Intermittent or persistent HIGH Pressure vent alarm
  • Intermittent or persistent drop in pulse oximeter reading

Follow-Up Plan to Prevent Recurrence

  • Notify PCP and Clinical Supervisor
  • Determine when trach tube was last changed
  • Change trach tube (if due) once patient’s condition stabilized
  • Consider the temperature of humidification
  • Collaborate with PCP/team/family to revise care plan, as needed

Accidental Decannulation / Dislodgement

The displacement of the tracheostomy tube that results in either its partial or complete removal out of the stoma.

Contributing Factors

  • Loose fitting trach ties
  • Need to upsize because the trach tube shaft is too short in length
  • Patient coughing
  • High level of patient activity (rolling in bed, crawling or ambulation)
  • Patient accidentally pulls out own trach tube (young child or delayed)
  • Trach tube catches on shirt during dressing changes
  • Increased traction on ventilator tubing pulls on trach tube

Symptoms

  • Low Pressure Alarm on Ventilator
  • Audible vocalization
  • Intermittent or sudden drop in pulse oximeter reading
  • Increased WOB
  • Cyanosis

Follow-Up Plan to Prevent Recurrence

  • Notify PCP and Clinical Supervisor
  • Determine when trach tube was last changed
  • Change trach tube (if due) once patient’s condition stabilized
  • Consider the temperature of humidification
  • Collaborate with PCP/team/family to revise care plan, as needed
